Recipe of Idly

Discription

Idly (or Idli) is a traditional South Indian dish made from a fermented batter of rice and urad dal (black gram), steamed into soft, fluffy, round cakes. It’s one of the healthiest and most popular breakfast items in India — light, nutritious, and easy to digest.Idly (or Idli) is a traditional South Indian dish made from a fermented batter of rice and urad dal (black gram), steamed into soft, fluffy, round cakes. It’s one of the healthiest and most popular breakfast items in India — light, nutritious, and easy to digest.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup urad dal (split black gram)
  • 2 cups idli rice (or regular rice)
  • 1/2 tsp fenugreek seeds (optional, but helps in fermentation)
  • Salt to taste
  • Water (as needed)

Steps

  1. Ingredients: Soak idli rice and urad dal separately for 6-8 hours or overnight.
  2. Grind the Batter: Grind the soaked urad dal into a smooth paste and idli rice into a slightly coarse paste. Mix both in a bowl and add salt.
  3. Ferment the Batter: Cover the batter and let it ferment for 8-12 hours in a warm place.
  4. Prepare Idli Molds: Grease the idli molds, pour the fermented batter into the molds, and set them in a steamer.
  5. Steam the Idlis: Steam for 10-12 minutes until cooked. Serve hot with chutney or sambar.
