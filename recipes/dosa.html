Recipe of Dosa

Description

Dosa is a popular South Indian dish that is a thin, crispy pancake made from a fermented batter of rice and urad dal (split black gram). It's light, flavorful, and pairs wonderfully with chutneys, sambar, or even a simple potato filling (masala dosa). Itâ€™s a staple breakfast dish in many Indian households, often enjoyed with a cup of steaming hot filter coffee

Ingredients

  • 1 cup rice (preferably short-grain or dosa rice)
  • 1/4 cup urad dal (split black gram)
  • 1/2 teaspoon fenugreek seeds (optional, helps in fermentation)
  • Water (for soaking and grinding)
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Oil or ghee (for cooking)

Steps

  1. Soak 1 cup rice, 1/4 cup urad dal, and 1/2 tsp fenugreek seeds for 4-6 hours or overnight.
  2. Grind the soaked ingredients with water to form a smooth batter, thick but pourable.
  3. Ferment the batter for 8-12 hours in a warm place until it rises and becomes slightly tangy.
  4. Heat a tawa (griddle), grease with oil or ghee, and spread a ladle of batter thinly in a circular motion.
  5. Cook for 1-2 minutes until golden and crispy, then serve hot with chutney or sambar.
